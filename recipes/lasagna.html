Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich, savory fillings. Typically, it features a hearty meat sauce (such as Bolognese), creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and a generous topping of melted mozzarella or Parmesan. Baked until bubbling and golden, lasagna is known for its comforting flavors and satisfying, layered texture. It’s a beloved comfort food that brings together bold herbs, slow-cooked sauces, and tender pasta in every bite.

Traditional Lasagna Recipe
Ingredients:

For the meat sauce:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for sautéing)

Instructions:

  1. Make the sauce:

    In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and sauté until soft. Add garlic and cook briefly. Stir in ground beef and cook until browned. Drain excess fat. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Let simmer for 20-30 minutes

  2. Prepare cheese mixture:

    In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan, salt, pepper, and optional parsley. Set aside.

  3. Cook noodles (if needed):

    If using traditional lasagna noodles, cook according to package directions. Drain and lay flat on a towel.

  4. Assemble lasagna:

    • Spread a thin layer of meat sauce at the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
    • Add a layer of noodles.
    • Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture.
    • Sprinkle with mozzarella.
    • Repeat layers until ingredients are used up, finishing with sauce and mozzarella on top. Sprinkle with Parmesan.

  5. Bake:

    Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 15-20 minutes, until golden and bubbly.

  6. Rest before serving:

    Let lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing. This helps it set and makes serving easier.
